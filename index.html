The Experience

The Husky Bot will be your guide. It'll assign teams, keep score, and trigger the end of the tournament. Just make sure you join the Main Lobby voice call in the Discord channel.

1. Join the Voice Call "Main Lobby" on Discord

Everyone hangs out here between games.

2. Mark Your Status

You're "Ready" by default. Use /set-status idle to voluntarily sit out (or tell someone else to mark your status for you).

Players who sit out for a game get automatic priority for the next game. Grab a beverage, watch the stream, fix your controls, etc.

3. Teams Are Shuffled

Husky Bot creates balanced 4v4 teams from the pool of ready players (using net flag differential).

If there are more than 8 ready players, some will randomly sit out.

4. Breakout Channels

Husky Bot gives each team a name and moves each team into a private voice channel.

5. Strategize

Teams have a few moments to talk strategy before the game starts.

6. Play the Game

Coordinate flag runs, call out enemies, avoid spawn traps, and work together!

7. Return to Lobby

After the match, everyone is automatically moved back to the main call.

8. Leaderboard Updates

Husky Bot will post the current standings then begin the next shuffle.

9. The Homestretch

In the final half-hour of tournament play, the "homestretch" event will be triggered and the final 3 games will be played.

The top 2 players on the leaderboard (with a "ready" status) will be promoted to Homestretch Captains. These 2 players will automatically be included in the final 3 games (on opposite teams). Their teammates will still be randomized.

The scoreboard is still live during the Homestretch, and there is no guarantee the Homestretch Captains finish in the top 2.

Your teammates change. Your mission doesn't.

Each flag your team captures adds to your score.

Each flag the enemy captures subtracts from it.

Stack wins. Minimize losses.

Scoring

Only the final game score matters on the leaderboard. Play to win, not to pad stats.

FAQ

Do I need to be good at Halo to join?

Nope! This is basically just an excuse to get together and game. The team balancing system will TRY to create fair matchups (based on each player's flag differential score), but the game mode is so chaotic that every player will be a part of big moments.

What if I need to take a break?

Use /set-status idle in the Discord chat and you'll be skipped in the next shuffle. Mark yourself ready when you want back in. Or just tell G.

Can I join late or leave early?

Yes! Come and go as you please. Just play at least 5 games to qualify for prizes.

How do I install Halo Infinite?

On Xbox: Press the Xbox button, go to the Store, search for "Halo Infinite," and download it (it's free).

On PC: Download it for free from Steam or the Xbox App.

Important: Sign in at least a day early to download any updates if you haven't played in a while.

How do I join the Discord?

On PC: Download Discord from discord.com/download, install it, create an account (or sign in), and join our server at discord.gg/SuCNdEVJbf.

On Xbox: Discord is already built in! Download the Xbox app on your phone, go to Settings â†’ Account â†’ Linked social accounts â†’ Link Discord. Then join our server at discord.gg/SuCNdEVJbf on your phone. Open Discord on your Xbox and our server will appear.

Tip: You can join a Discord voice call just like you're joining an Xbox Party. Join the "Main Lobby" channel and Husky Bot will handle the rest.

Why aren't we playing Halo 5 Husky Raid?

We all know that H5 Husky is peak Husky. The req-based weapons, the maps, the public API...

But Halo Infinite has a few key advantages:

  • It's cross-platform (Xbox + PC)
  • It's free to play
  • Chan doesn't have to remember the complicated Halo 5 controls

Can I suggest a different map, or changes to the tournament format?

Sure! A few details are likely to change as we get closer to the event. And maps are totally up for debate still. But the Husky Bot is nearly ready -- so the earlier you make a suggestion, the more likely we can accomplish it.

I'm busy. Do I need to commit in advance to play in the tournament?

Not exactly, but early commitments help with planning, and they may or may not come with a bonus gift. And definitely install/update the game in advance to avoid annoyance on tournament day.

Can I practice somewhere?

I think there's a casual playlist on Halo Infinite that has Husky Raid games in it. Action Sack or something?
